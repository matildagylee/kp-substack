02
Positioning

What this is

A weekly newsletter for women who want to make better choices about what they consume - fashion, beauty, food - but don't know where to start and don't have unlimited budgets.

What this is NOT

  • Not an eco-warrior guilt trip
  • Not sponsored content disguised as advice

Where it could go

Could evolve into a destination for women who already identify as eco-conscious.

"The Curious Pragmatist"

A woman in her late 20s to early 40s who:

  • Knows fast fashion and cheap cosmetics aren't great, but defaults to them out of convenience
  • Wants to do better but feels overwhelmed by conflicting info
  • Cares about her wallet as much as her values
  • Wants honest, personal, tried-and-tested guidance

Voice & Tone

First-person, conversational Honest about trade-offs European perspective, North American relatability Budget-transparent Like texting a friend who did the research
03
Launch Post
LinkedIn copy for Kristina's announcement
LinkedIn

I spent the better part of a decade being the definition of overconsumption. Fast fashion hauls. Whatever skincare was trending. Food choices driven entirely by convenience.

And I didn't think twice about any of it.

Then I moved to London. And something shifted.

I started noticing how differently people consume here. Smaller wardrobes. Local markets. Ingredient lists that don't read like a chemistry exam. It wasn't performative or preachy. It was just... normal.

And once I saw it, I couldn't unsee it.

So I started doing the homework. Testing things out. Swapping products - not all at once, but slowly. What actually works? What's worth the price? What's just greenwashing in better packaging?

Turns out, a lot of "better choices" are also just... better. Better quality. Better value over time. Sometimes even cheaper.

The Conscious Edit is where I share what I've learned.

No affiliate links. Just honest, personal experience from someone with a full-time job who doesn't have unlimited time or money to throw at this stuff.

If you've ever wanted to shop cleaner, eat better, or just understand what you're actually putting on your body - but didn't know where to start - this is for you.

6-Month Growth Strategy
1
Foundation
Month 1-2
"Build the engine"
Goal: 200 subscribers, 10 posts, voice established
Content
  • Publish 4 posts before announcing publicly (backlog creates credibility)
  • Week 1-2: Write and schedule first 4 posts across all pillars
  • Week 3: Soft launch to friends/family (50-100 people)
  • Week 4: Public launch with LinkedIn post
  • Month 2: Settle into weekly Tuesday cadence
Growth Tactics
  • Personal outreach: Kristina DMs 10 people/day from her network
  • LinkedIn: 2x/week short posts that tease newsletter content
  • Instagram: 3 posts/week (carousel tips, behind-the-scenes)
  • Cross-post on Substack Notes daily
  • Add Substack link to all social bios, email signature
Ops Setup
  • Substack profile with professional headshot and compelling "About" page
  • Choose 3-5 Substack categories/tags for discoverability
  • Canva template for newsletter header + social posts
  • Content calendar (Google Sheet or Notion)
Milestone: 200 subscribers by end of Month 2
2
Community
Month 3-4
"Find your people"
Goal: 500 subscribers, engaged comments, first collaborations
Content Evolution
  • Introduce "The Swap" as a recurring bi-weekly segment
  • Start "Label Decoder" monthly series
  • First reader Q&A post
  • One "deep dive" long-form post per month
Growth Tactics
  • Substack recommendations: Reach out to 10-15 complementary writers for mutual recs
  • Guest features: 1-2 sustainable brand founders for Q&A posts
  • LinkedIn: Increase to 3x/week, engage in comments on related creators
  • Instagram Reels: 1x/week short video comparisons
  • Referral program: Substack's built-in referral (3 friends = shoutout)
  • Engagement: Reply to every comment. Ask questions. Feature reader responses.
Milestone: 500 subscribers, 15%+ open rate, 5+ comments/post
3
Authority
Month 5-6
"Become the go-to"
Goal: 1,000+ subscribers, monetization foundations
Content Evolution
  • Publish a "Best of" comprehensive guide (high shareability)
  • Seasonal content: "Spring Capsule Wardrobe: 15 Pieces, Under £500"
  • Start tagging/reviewing brands - some will reshare, driving organic reach
  • One "controversial take" post to spark conversation
Growth Tactics
  • Podcast guesting: Pitch 3-5 podcasts in conscious living/wellness space
  • Substack leaderboard: Coordinate a "recommend me" week with writer network
  • Welcome sequence: 3 automated emails for new subscribers
  • SEO: Optimize post titles for search (Substack posts rank well)
Monetization Prep
  • Survey readers: "Would you pay £5/month for premium content?"
  • Test one premium post behind paywall to gauge conversion
  • Explore affiliate partnerships ONLY with personally-used brands
  • Build a "brand partnership" one-pager (editorial control, honest reviews only)
Milestone: 1,000 subscribers, 20%+ open rate, monetization plan validated

09
Risks & Mitigations
Burnout from weekly publishing
Batch-write 2-3 posts at a time; keep a 2-week buffer of scheduled content.
"Just another sustainability newsletter"
Lean into the anti-expert, budget-conscious, no-sponsorship angle - that IS the differentiator.
Slow early growth
Normal. Most Substacks hit inflection at 6-12 months. Focus on quality + consistency over vanity metrics.
Pressure to accept sponsorships
The "no sponsorship" positioning IS the brand. If monetizing, do paid subscriptions first, affiliate only for personally-used products.
Content scope too broad
Rotate pillars weekly so each gets regular coverage without overwhelming Kristina.
