Positioning

What this is

A weekly newsletter for women who want to make better choices about what they consume - fashion, beauty, food - but don't know where to start and don't have unlimited budgets.

What this is NOT

  • Not an academic or research publication - it's personal experience, not journalism
  • Not sponsored content (today) - but sponsorships and brand partnerships are a future monetization path, done transparently and on Kristina's terms

Where it could go

Could evolve into a destination for women who already identify as eco-conscious.

"The Curious Pragmatist"

A woman in her late 20s to early 40s who:

  • Knows fast fashion and cheap cosmetics aren't great, but defaults to them out of convenience
  • Wants to do better but feels overwhelmed by conflicting info
  • Cares about her wallet as much as her values
  • Wants honest, personal, tried-and-tested guidance

Voice & Tone

First-person, conversational Honest about trade-offs European perspective, North American relatability Budget-transparent Like texting a friend who did the research

I spent the better part of a decade being the definition of overconsumption.

Fast fashion hauls. Whatever skincare was trending. Food choices driven entirely by convenience.

And I didn't think twice about any of it.

Then I moved to London.

I started noticing how differently people consume here. Smaller wardrobes. Local markets (which I'm absolutely obsessed with). Ingredient lists that don't read like a chemistry exam. It wasn't performative or preachy. This was the norm.

So I started doing the homework. Testing things out. Swapping products - not all at once, but slowly. What actually works? What's worth the price? What's just greenwashing in better packaging?

Turns out, a lot of "better choices" are also just... better. Better quality. Better value over time. Sometimes even cheaper.

The Conscious Edit is where I want to share what I've learned.

Honest, personal experience from someone with a full-time job who doesn't have unlimited time or money to throw at this stuff.

If you've ever wanted to shop cleaner, eat better, or just understand what you're actually putting on your body - but didn't know where to start - this is for you.

Let me do the homework so you don't have to.

xx KP

Pre-launch teaser - the origin story

Founder & Builder | Writing about what I'm learning along the way

I've been thinking a lot about what kind of business I want to build.

Not the "what makes money" kind of thinking. The "what would I actually want to exist in the world" kind.

And it started with my friend's perfume.

She was spending serious money on fragrance. Not cheap stuff. High-end, beautifully packaged, the works. She could afford the best.

But when I looked at what was actually in those bottles? Phthalates. Synthetic musks that accumulate in your body. Ingredients hidden behind a single word on the label: "fragrance."

She had no idea. None.

And that's the thing that stuck with me. It wasn't that she didn't care. It wasn't that she couldn't afford better. She just didn't know. Nobody had ever told her. Nobody had done the homework for her.

And I realised - that's most of us.

We're not making bad choices because we're lazy or don't care. We're making them because the information is buried, the marketing is confusing, and honestly? Who has the time to decode ingredient lists after a 10-hour workday?

That moment with my friend is what planted the seed for something I'm building.

It's not a product. It's not an app. It's something much simpler.

I want to be the friend who does the homework. The one who reads the labels, tests the alternatives, tracks the prices, and tells you honestly: is it worth it or not?

Fashion. Beauty. Food. The everyday stuff we all buy without thinking.

More on this soon. But if this resonates - if you've ever wanted someone to just tell you straight what's actually good and what's just good marketing - you're exactly who I'm building this for.

xx KP

09
Risks & Mitigations
Burnout from weekly publishing
Batch-write 2-3 posts at a time; keep a 2-week buffer of scheduled content.
"Just another sustainability newsletter"
Lean into the anti-expert, budget-conscious, no-sponsorship angle - that IS the differentiator.
Slow early growth
Normal. Most Substacks hit inflection at 6-12 months. Focus on quality + consistency over vanity metrics.
Pressure to accept sponsorships
The "no sponsorship" positioning IS the brand. If monetizing, do paid subscriptions first, affiliate only for personally-used products.
Content scope too broad
Rotate pillars weekly so each gets regular coverage without overwhelming Kristina.
