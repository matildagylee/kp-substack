01
Brand Essence

The Conscious Edit helps women make better choices about what they consume - fashion, beauty, food - with honest reviews, real budgets, and zero gatekeeping. It's the homework you don't have time to do, from someone who actually did it.

Mission

Make conscious consumption accessible, affordable, and honest. No guilt trips. No luxury gatekeeping. Just clear information so you can decide for yourself.

Audience

"The Curious Pragmatist" - women in their late 20s to early 40s who want to do better but feel overwhelmed by conflicting info, limited budgets, and marketing noise.

Positioning

Not an eco-warrior publication. Not a luxury lifestyle brand. A newsletter by a real person with a real job sharing what she's actually tried, what it actually cost, and whether it was actually worth it.

Promise

Every product mentioned has been personally tested. Every price is listed. No affiliate links, no sponsorships, no hidden incentives. If something isn't worth it, we'll say so.

Primary use

Forest green for CTAs, links, category labels, and the "CE" monogram. Never use it for large background areas - keep it as a precise accent.

Backgrounds

White (#fff) for article content. Parchment for callout boxes and comparison cards. Linen for alternative page backgrounds. Never use green as a page background.

Text

Ink for headlines. Charcoal for body copy. Stone for metadata, dates, and captions. Never use pure black (#000) - it's too harsh.

Clay (red)

Reserved strictly for ingredient concern flags and "don't" examples. Never use as a decorative colour or for CTAs.

05
Voice & Tone

Kristina's voice is the brand. First person, conversational, honest about trade-offs. Like texting a friend who's done the research for you.

Honest

Always acknowledge trade-offs. If something costs more, say so. If a product didn't work, say that too. Readers trust us because we don't sugarcoat.

Personal

First person. "I tried this." "I spent this much." Real experiences, not aggregated reviews. The newsletter IS Kristina's perspective.

Accessible

No jargon without explanation. Always include prices. Never assume the reader has prior knowledge about sustainability, ingredients, or certifications.

Non-judgmental

Never shame the reader for their current choices. "Here's what I found" not "here's what you should do." The reader decides.

Do write like this

  • "I've had these trousers for 18 months. They still look new."
  • "It costs more. Here's why I think it's worth it - or isn't."
  • "This is the honest trade-off: it fades faster. You'll need to reapply."
  • "I'm not a toxicologist. But here's what the research says."
  • "Your calculation might be different. That's fine."

Don't write like this

  • "You NEED to switch to clean beauty immediately."
  • "This revolutionary product will transform your routine."
  • "Top 10 sustainable brands you'll LOVE."
  • "If you care about the planet, you should..."
  • "This game-changing, must-have essential..."

07
Photography Direction

Warm, natural light. Real settings, not studios. Products as Kristina actually uses them - on a bathroom shelf, a kitchen counter, a bedside table. Imperfect and lived-in.

Flat lay: products on marble or wood surface
Close-up: ingredient label or texture detail
Lifestyle: product in real context (shelf, bag, vanity)

Photo style

  • Natural daylight, warm tones
  • Real environments (home, shop, market)
  • Products shown in use or context
  • Muted, earthy colour grading
  • Slightly imperfect compositions

Avoid

  • Studio-lit product shots on white
  • Over-saturated or filtered images
  • Stock photography feel
  • Perfect, symmetrical flat lays
  • Anything that looks like an ad
08
Content Pillars

Four pillars structure all content. Each has a category label colour-coded with Forest green. Pillars rotate weekly in the Tuesday Drop.

Closet

Fashion & wardrobe. Sustainable brands, capsule wardrobes, fast fashion alternatives, charity shop finds, cost-per-wear analysis.

Counter

Beauty & skincare. Ingredient breakdowns, clean swaps, label decoding, product reviews with full transparency on what's inside.

Cart

Food & consumption. Healthy eating, meal planning, label reading, seasonal shopping, understanding what "natural" actually means.

The Swap

Recurring comparison format. One mainstream product vs. one conscious alternative. Honest on price, quality, trade-offs. No winner declared - reader decides.
